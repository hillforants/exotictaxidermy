# Data Management Plan

**Project Overview**

Our theme for our collection is exotic taxidermy from the Victorian Era (1837-1901). For this project, we are using CollectionBuilder, GitHub, and Google Sheets. Our project functions as a conglomerate of exotic taxidermy specimens, and how different animals are chosen to be represented in Victorian displays. The intended audience of this collection is anyone curious about the history of taxidermy or researchers focused on Victorian furniture preference and collection habits. This collection sheds light on the morbid technology of taxidermy during this time period, and how taxidermist pioneers created the taxidermist culture that our modern advancements stem from today. 

**Roles and Responsibilities**

The creators of this project are students Harley Antflick and Olivia Heffernan. We were able to split our responsibilities proactively, making sure that we both had a hefty hand in the production of this collection. Antflick contributed in the creation and customization of the Github coding, and the introduction of themes into the website. Heffernan contributed to the informative metadata present in the website, and how pieces are presented in our collection. Together, we created a solid collection of works that represented both of our efforts toward Victorian taxidermy exposure.

**Data Inventory and Provenance**

For this project, we used various sources from notable taxidermist research websites, as well as accredited research facilities and archives with historical imagery of the preparation of taxidermy, and the displaying of taxidermy, including the Smithsonian Institute and the Digital Archive of America.

**File Inventory + Tools and Access**

All of our files are images (jpeg or png). To reproduce or work with our data, someone only needs access to CollectionBuilder, Github, Google Sheets, Google Docs, and Google Drive.

**Storage and Stewardship**

As the term continues, our project lives in the public realm, accessible by anyone who is interested. Following the term, the project will remain in the public realm, as we would like anyone to be able to access the information after we have finished this collection. The files for this project are stored in Google Drive, GitHub, and Google Sheets. These files are able to be accessed and edited by creators Olivia Heffernan and Harley Antflick, but the CollectionBuilder website and Github repository are able to be viewed by the public. Maintaining this project would mean adding additional imagery, upkeep of information if information is updated or revealed, as well as edits to code in the event that code shifts over time.This project will be maintained for the purpose of this class, and will likely not be edited after the termination of this term. If GitHub Pages stops serving the site and repository, the site will likely remain terminated. 

**Access, Reuse, and Rights**

The public may use our repository and CollectionBuilder has references in the future under certain circumstances. In order to utilize our collection, the individual or group must credit the original creators (Harley Antflick and Olivia Heffernan), as well as properly citing the resource that they use to the original source. If an individual or group were to use imagery sourced from the Smithsonian Institute, we would like to be credited, along with the proper citations for the Smithsonian Institute. All imagery utilized for this collection have been cited in CollectionBuilder and do not include strict usage policies, other than general citation. Individuals and groups should consider the ethical nature of taxidermy, especially exotic taxidermy from this time period. In many instances, animal abuse may be present in the process of creation, and could offend certain audiences that may be sensitive to posthumous animal imagery. Individuals or groups should warn audiences of the nature of the imagery, and that the animals showcased in the collection are deceased.
